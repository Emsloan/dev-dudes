Don't use "AI-powered" or lead with AI in client-facing copy.
**Why:** Creative clients (musicians, makers, small businesses) tend to be anti-AI. Mixed connotations in this niche.
**How to apply:** Describe Elliott's work as problem-solving, engineering, validating — not AI-driven.

Don't use closing/sales language on the client-facing page ("closes deals", "closes clients").
**Why:** Reads as predatory to the very people you're trying to win over.
**How to apply:** Frame Austin's sales background as a client benefit — he makes you feel taken care of, not sold to.

Elliott designs, tests, and validates — he does not "write code."
**Why:** He uses agentic development; describing him as a coder is technically inaccurate.
**How to apply:** Use "solves it, validates it, ships it" framing rather than "writes the code."

The founder portraits are AI-generated (Nano Banana img2img), not drawn by Elliott.
**Why:** Elliott is an illustrator but did not make these specific images.
**How to apply:** Don't attribute the illustrations to him.

Brand voice: informal, direct, a bit cheeky. Short punchy labels ("The engineer", "The rainmaker") over full bios. Copy that sounds like two humans, not an agency.
